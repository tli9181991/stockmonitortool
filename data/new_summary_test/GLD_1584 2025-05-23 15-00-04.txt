Norway stocks higher at close of trade; Oslo OBX up 0.16%
Investing.com – Norway stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the Healthcare Equipment & Services, Pharma Biotech & Life Sciences and Utilities sectors led shares higher. At the close in Oslo, the Oslo OBX added 0.16%. The best performers of the session on the Oslo OBX were  Frontline Ltd  (OL:FRO), which rose 2.71% or 4.80 points to trade at 182.25 at the close. Meanwhile, Equinor ASA (OL:EQNR) added 0.88% or 2.10 points to end at 239.80 and Mowi ASA (OL:MOWI) was up 0.86% or 1.60 points to 188.60 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Nordic Semiconductor ASA  (OL:NOD), which fell 3.18% or 4.00 points to trade at 121.80 at the close.  Golden Ocean  Group Ltd (OL:GOGL) declined 2.75% or 2.05 points to end at 72.55 and  Norwegian Air Shuttle ASA  (OL:NAS) was down 2.24% or 0.30 points to 13.08. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Oslo Stock Exchange by 143 to 131 and 27 ended unchanged. Crude oil for July delivery was up 0.47% or 0.29 to $61.49 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in July rose 0.37% or 0.24 to hit $64.68 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 1.95% or 64.30 to trade at $3,359.30 a troy ounce. EUR/NOK was down 0.21% to 11.47, while USD/NOK fell 0.79% to 10.11. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.69% at 99.16.
