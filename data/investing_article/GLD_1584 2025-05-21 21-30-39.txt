Mexico stocks higher at close of trade; S&P/BMV IPC up 0.44%
Investing.com – Mexico stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Industrials, Consumer Goods & Services and Consumer Staples sectors led shares higher. At the close in Mexico, the S&P/BMV IPC gained 0.44% to hit a new 52-week high. The best performers of the session on the S&P/BMV IPC were Industrias  Penoles  Sab De CV (BMV:PEOLES), which rose 3.32% or 13.57 points to trade at 422.68 at the close. Meanwhile, Coca-Cola Femsa SAB de CV (BMV:KOFUBL) added 2.91% or 5.24 points to end at 185.00 and Grupo Aeroportuario Del Sureste (BMV:ASURB) was up 1.76% or 11.53 points to 668.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Megacable Holdings, S.A.B. De C.V. (BMV:MEGACPO), which fell 5.78% or 3.11 points to trade at 50.68 at the close. Gentera SAB de CV (BMV:GENTERA) declined 1.95% or 0.76 points to end at 38.15 and GCC SAB de CV (BMV:GCC) was down 1.58% or 2.83 points to 176.53. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Mexico Stock Exchange by 140 to 93 and 22 ended unchanged. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.98% or 32.15 to $3,316.75 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July fell 1.10% or 0.68 to hit $61.35 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 0.96% or 0.63 to trade at $64.75 a barrel.
