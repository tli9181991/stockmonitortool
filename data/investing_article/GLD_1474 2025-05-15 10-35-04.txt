Investing.com – India stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Real Estate, Auto and Metals sectors led shares higher. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 gained 1.60% to hit a new 6-months high, while the BSE Sensex 30 index climbed 1.48%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  Hero MotoCorp  Ltd (NSE:HROM), which rose 6.29% or 255.70 points to trade at 4,322.90 at the close. Meanwhile,  JSW Steel  Ltd (NSE:JSTL) added 4.66% or 46.10 points to end at 1,035.40 and  Tata Motors Ltd  (NSE:TAMO) was up 4.23% or 29.60 points to 728.55 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Britannia Industries  Ltd (NSE:BRIT), which fell 0.22% or 12.00 points to trade at 5,472.00 at the close.  IndusInd Bank  Ltd. (NSE:INBK) declined 0.17% or 1.35 points to end at 780.00 and  Coal India  Ltd (NSE:COAL) was up 0.10% or 0.40 points to 403.50. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were Tata Motors Ltd (BO:TAMO) which rose 4.16% to 728.10, HCL Technologies Ltd (BO:HCLT) which was up 3.37% to settle at 1,692.90 and Eternal Ltd (BO:ETEA) which gained 2.22% to close at 242.05. The worst performers were IndusInd Bank Ltd. (BO:INBK) which was down 0.24% to 779.70 in late trade, Hindustan Unilever Ltd. (BO:HLL) which gained 0.16% to settle at 2,355.00 and Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. (BO:KTKM) which was up 0.50% to 2,102.80 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 1651 to 711 and 39 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 2502 rose and 1307 declined, while 145 ended unchanged. The India VIX, which measures the implied volatility of Nifty 50 options, was down 2.86% to 16.73. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 0.25% or 7.95 to $3,180.35 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 3.56% or 2.25 to hit $60.90 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 3.37% or 2.23 to trade at $63.86 a barrel.
