Investing.com – Finland stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Basic Materials, Utilities and Healthcare sectors led shares higher. At the close in Helsinki, the OMX Helsinki 25 gained 2.49%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Helsinki 25 were Outokumpu Oyj (HE:OUT1V), which rose 4.61% or 0.14 points to trade at 3.18 at the close. Meanwhile,  Stora Enso  Oyj R (HE:STERV) added 3.90% or 0.29 points to end at 7.68 and Metso Oyj (HE:METSO) was up 3.71% or 0.30 points to 8.50 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Elisa Oyj (HE:ELISA), which rose 0.99% or 0.44 points to trade at 44.90 at the close.  Kojamo  (HE:KOJAMO) added 1.03% or 0.10 points to end at 9.32 and  Nokian Renkaat  Oyj (HE:TYRES) was up 1.34% or 0.09 points to 6.43. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Helsinki Stock Exchange by 142 to 33 and 13 ended unchanged. Brent oil for June delivery was down 0.43% or 0.28 to $64.48 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 0.67% or 0.41 to hit $61.09 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract fell 0.84% or 27.26 to trade at $3,217.34 a troy ounce.
