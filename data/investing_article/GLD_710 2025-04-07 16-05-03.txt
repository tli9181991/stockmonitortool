Investing.com – Belgium stocks were lower after the close on Monday, as losses in the Utilities, Healthcare and Consumer Services sectors led shares lower. At the close in Brussels, the BEL 20 fell 3.99% to hit a new 6-months low. The best performers of the session on the BEL 20 were  Solvay  SA (EBR:SOLB), which fell 0.66% or 0.20 points to trade at 30.12 at the close. Meanwhile,  Sofina  (EBR:SOF) fell 1.22% or 2.60 points to end at 210.60 and  Cofinimmo  SA (EBR:COFB) was down 1.95% or 1.15 points to 57.95 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Elia (EBR:ELI), which fell 7.26% or 6.10 points to trade at 77.90 at the close. Groep Brussel Lambert NV (EBR:GBLB) declined 6.54% or 4.40 points to end at 62.90 and  Umicore  SA (EBR:UMI) was down 6.04% or 0.50 points to 7.70. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Brussels Stock Exchange by 91 to 10 and 5 ended unchanged. Shares in Groep Brussel Lambert NV (EBR:GBLB) fell to 5-year lows; down 6.54% or 4.40 to 62.90. Shares in Umicore SA (EBR:UMI) fell to 5-year lows; falling 6.04% or 0.50 to 7.70.  Gold Futures for June delivery was down 1.28% or 38.90 to $2,996.50 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 2.13% or 1.32 to hit $60.67 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract fell 2.09% or 1.37 to trade at $64.21 a barrel. EUR/USD was unchanged 0.40% to 1.09, while EUR/GBP rose 0.98% to 0.86. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.49% at 103.27.
