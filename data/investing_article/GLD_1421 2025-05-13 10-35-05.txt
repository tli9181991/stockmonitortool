Investing.com – India stocks were lower after the close on Tuesday, as losses in the Technology, Power and Auto sectors led shares lower. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 fell 1.39%, while the BSE Sensex 30 index lost 1.55%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  Bharat Electronics  Ltd (NSE:BAJE), which rose 4.07% or 13.15 points to trade at 335.95 at the close. Meanwhile,  Hero MotoCorp  Ltd (NSE:HROM) added 1.83% or 73.10 points to end at 4,058.00 and Sun Pharmaceutical Industries Ltd. (NSE:SUN) was up 0.88% or 14.80 points to 1,701.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Infosys  Ltd (NSE:INFY), which fell 3.63% or 59.00 points to trade at 1,567.90 at the close. Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd (NSE:PGRD) declined 3.19% or 9.85 points to end at 299.20 and  HCL Technologies  Ltd (NSE:HCLT) was down 3.01% or 50.30 points to 1,620.00. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were Sun Pharmaceutical Industries Ltd. (BO:SUN) which rose 0.99% to 1,703.00, Adani Port and Special Economic Zone Ltd (BO:APSE) which was up 0.46% to settle at 1,369.20 and Bajaj Finance Ltd (BO:BJFN) which gained 0.28% to close at 9,042.00. The worst performers were Infosys Ltd (BO:INFY) which was down 3.57% to 1,568.60 in late trade, Eternal Ltd (BO:ETEA) which lost 3.48% to settle at 231.50 and Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd (BO:PGRD) which was down 3.22% to 299.10 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 1656 to 713 and 33 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 2506 rose and 1307 declined, while 134 ended unchanged. Shares in Bharat Electronics Ltd (NSE:BAJE) rose to all time highs; rising 4.07% or 13.15 to 335.95.  The India VIX, which measures the implied volatility of Nifty 50 options, was down 1.29% to 18.16. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.91% or 29.36 to $3,257.36 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June rose 0.86% or 0.53 to hit $62.48 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract rose 0.74% or 0.48 to trade at $65.44 a barrel.
