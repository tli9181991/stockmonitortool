Investing.com – Turkey stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Banking, Telecoms and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Istanbul, the BIST 100 added 1.36% to hit a new 6-months high. The best performers of the session on the BIST 100 were  Turk Traktor  ve Ziraat Makineleri AS (IS:TTRAK), which rose 6.23% or 48.50 points to trade at 827.50 at the close. Meanwhile,  Tekfen Holding  AS (IS:TKFEN) added 5.27% or 4.30 points to end at 85.95 and Turkiye  Garanti Bankasi  (IS:GARAN) was up 5.05% or 6.90 points to 143.50 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Sdt Uzay ve Savunma Teknolojileri  AS (IS:SDTTR), which fell 9.04% or 20.00 points to trade at 201.30 at the close.  Reeder Teknoloji Sanayi Ve Ticaret  AS (IS:REEDR) declined 8.83% or 1.22 points to end at 12.60 and  Altinay Savunma Teknolojileri AS  (IS:ALTNY) was down 4.41% or 4.00 points to 86.70. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Istanbul Stock Exchange by 303 to 272 and 16 ended unchanged. Shares in Tekfen Holding AS (IS:TKFEN) rose to all time highs; up 5.27% or 4.30 to 85.95. Shares in Turkiye Garanti Bankasi (IS:GARAN) rose to all time highs; up 5.05% or 6.90 to 143.50.  Gold Futures for April delivery was up 0.41% or 11.99 to $2,932.89 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April rose 2.20% or 1.46 to hit $67.71 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract rose 1.93% or 1.34 to trade at $70.90 a barrel. USD/TRY was up 0.09% to 36.59, while EUR/TRY rose 0.20% to 40.00. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.12% at 103.40.
