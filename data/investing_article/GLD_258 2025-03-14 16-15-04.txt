Investing.com – Morocco stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the Pharmaceutical Industry, Real Estate and Banking sectors led shares higher. At the close in Casablanca, the Moroccan All Shares rose 0.75%. The best performers of the session on the Moroccan All Shares were Stokvis Nord Afrique (CSE:SNA), which rose 9.86% or 2.55 points to trade at 28.40 at the close. Meanwhile, Ste de Travaux de Realisation d’Ouvrages et de Constuction Industielle SA (CSE:STR) added 4.44% or 2.00 points to end at 47.00 and  Aradei Capital  SA (CSE:ARD) was up 3.88% or 18.50 points to 495.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Miniere Touissit (CSE:CMT), which fell 3.26% or 58.00 points to trade at 1,720.00 at the close.  S2M  (CSE:S2M) declined 2.79% or 12.00 points to end at 418.00 and Marocaine Ste de Therapeutique SA (CSE:SOT) was down 2.37% or 26.00 points to 1,070.00. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Casablanca Stock Exchange by 36 to 14 and 7 ended unchanged. Shares in Stokvis Nord Afrique (CSE:SNA) rose to 5-year highs; up 9.86% or 2.55 to 28.40.  Crude oil for April delivery was up 0.75% or 0.50 to $67.05 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in May rose 0.73% or 0.51 to hit $70.39 a barrel, while the April Gold Futures contract rose 0.28% or 8.26 to trade at $2,999.56 a troy ounce. EUR/MAD was up 0.14% to 10.55, while USD/MAD fell 0.29% to 9.67. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.13% at 103.69.
