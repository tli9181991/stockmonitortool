Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Gold, Utilities and Industrials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 added 0.16%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Polynovo Ltd (ASX:PNV), which rose 11.45% or 0.15 points to trade at 1.46 at the close. Meanwhile, Orica Ltd (ASX:ORI) added 7.39% or 1.24 points to end at 18.01 and Platinum Asset Management Ltd (ASX:PTM) was up 6.84% or 0.04 points to 0.63 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Westpac Banking Corp (ASX:WBC), which fell 4.06% or 1.29 points to trade at 30.50 at the close.  Pilbara Minerals Ltd  (ASX:PLS) declined 3.90% or 0.06 points to end at 1.48 and Healius Ltd (ASX:HLS) was down 3.75% or 0.06 points to 1.54. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 644 to 438 and 445 ended unchanged. The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 2.83% to 12.48. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 1.62% or 55.06 to $3,336.84 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June rose 0.17% or 0.10 to hit $58.17 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract rose 0.08% or 0.05 to trade at $61.17 a barrel.
