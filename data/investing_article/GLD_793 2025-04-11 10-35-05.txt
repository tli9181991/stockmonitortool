Investing.com – India stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the Metals, Consumer Durables and Power sectors led shares higher. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 gained 1.92%, while the BSE Sensex 30 index gained 1.77%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  Hindalco Industries  Ltd. (NSE:HALC), which rose 6.70% or 37.80 points to trade at 601.80 at the close. Meanwhile,  JSW Steel  Ltd (NSE:JSTL) added 4.99% or 47.15 points to end at 992.70 and  Tata Steel  Ltd (NSE:TISC) was up 4.97% or 6.32 points to 133.50 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Asian Paints  Ltd. (NSE:ASPN), which fell 0.75% or 18.15 points to trade at 2,393.00 at the close.  Apollo Hospitals  Enterprises Ltd. (NSE:APLH) declined 0.53% or 36.10 points to end at 6,798.00 and  Tata Consultancy Services  Ltd. (NSE:TCS) was down 0.30% or 9.60 points to 3,237.00. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were Tata Steel Ltd (BO:TISC) which rose 4.87% to 133.40, Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd (BO:PGRD) which was up 3.72% to settle at 304.20 and NTPC Ltd (BO:NTPC) which gained 3.21% to close at 359.95. The worst performers were Asian Paints Ltd. (BO:ASPN) which was down 0.76% to 2,391.50 in late trade, Tata Consultancy Services Ltd. (BO:TCS) which lost 0.20% to settle at 3,239.60 and Hindustan Unilever Ltd. (BO:HLL) which was up 0.47% to 2,360.60 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 1953 to 411 and 38 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 2969 rose and 839 declined, while 119 ended unchanged. Shares in Tata Consultancy Services Ltd. (NSE:TCS) fell to 52-week lows; losing 0.30% or 9.60 to 3,237.00. Shares in Tata Consultancy Services Ltd. (BO:TCS) fell to 52-week lows; losing 0.20% or 6.50 to 3,239.60.  The India VIX, which measures the implied volatility of Nifty 50 options, was down 7.73% to 19.77. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 2.06% or 65.61 to $3,243.11 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May rose 0.58% or 0.35 to hit $60.42 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract rose 0.54% or 0.34 to trade at $63.67 a barrel.
