Investing.com – Italy stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Oil & Gas, Travel & Leisure and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Milan, the Investing.com Italy 40 gained 0.95%. The best performers of the session on the Investing.com Italy 40 were Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena SpA (BIT:BMPS), which rose 4.25% or 0.31 points to trade at 7.51 at the close. Meanwhile,  Saipem  SpA (BIT:SPMI) added 3.05% or 0.06 points to end at 2.13 and  Mediobanca  Banca di Credito Finanziario SpA (BIT:MDBI) was up 2.76% or 0.47 points to 17.51 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Brunello Cucinelli (BIT:BCU), which fell 2.62% or 2.90 points to trade at 107.70 at the close. Leonardo SpA (BIT:LDOF) declined 1.06% or 0.50 points to end at 46.70 and  Moncler SpA  (BIT:MONC) was down 0.85% or 0.52 points to 60.92. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Milan Stock Exchange by 347 to 196 and 40 ended unchanged. Shares in Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena SpA (BIT:BMPS) rose to 52-week highs; gaining 4.25% or 0.31 to 7.51.
