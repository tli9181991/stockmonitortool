Investing.com – Sweden stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Telecoms, Financials and Basic Materials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Stockholm, the OMX Stockholm 30 rose 2.68%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Stockholm 30 were Sinch AB (ST:SINCH), which rose 6.49% or 1.23 points to trade at 20.18 at the close. Meanwhile, Hexagon AB ser. B (ST:HEXAb) added 6.24% or 5.30 points to end at 90.00 and NIBE Industrier AB ser. B (ST:NIBEb) was up 5.19% or 1.80 points to 35.50 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Samhallsbyggnadsbolaget i Norden AB (ST:SBBb), which fell 0.14% or 0.01 points to trade at 3.63 at the close.  Essity AB B  (ST:ESSITYb) added 0.51% or 1.40 points to end at 277.90 and Evolution AB (ST:EVOG) was up 0.63% or 5.00 points to 795.20. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Stockholm Stock Exchange by 597 to 173 and 49 ended unchanged. Crude oil for May delivery was down 0.91% or 0.56 to $60.94 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in June fell 0.62% or 0.40 to hit $64.36 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract fell 0.81% or 26.39 to trade at $3,218.21 a troy ounce. EUR/SEK was unchanged 0.04% to 11.11, while USD/SEK unchanged 0.03% to 9.77. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.53% at 99.36.
