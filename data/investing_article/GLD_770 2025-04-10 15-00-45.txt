Investing.com – Norway stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Healthcare Equipment & Services, Pharma Biotech & Life Sciences and Utilities sectors led shares higher. At the close in Oslo, the Oslo OBX gained 2.90%. The best performers of the session on the Oslo OBX were  Golden Ocean  Group Ltd (OL:GOGL), which rose 6.20% or 4.30 points to trade at 73.70 at the close. Meanwhile,  Storebrand  ASA (OL:STB) added 4.14% or 4.70 points to end at 118.30 and Norsk Hydro ASA (OL:NHY) was up 3.90% or 2.02 points to 53.86 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Nel ASA (OL:NEL), which rose 0.34% or 0.01 points to trade at 2.33 at the close. Orkla ASA (OL:ORK) added 0.80% or 0.90 points to end at 113.30 and  Var Energi  ASA (OL:VAR) was up 1.39% or 0.38 points to 27.66. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Oslo Stock Exchange by 234 to 53 and 16 ended unchanged. Crude oil for May delivery was down 5.04% or 3.14 to $59.21 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in June fell 4.54% or 2.97 to hit $62.51 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 3.10% or 95.54 to trade at $3,174.94 a troy ounce. EUR/NOK was up 2.51% to 12.06, while USD/NOK rose 0.44% to 10.79. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 1.71% at 100.87.
