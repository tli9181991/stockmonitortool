Investing.com – India stocks were lower after the close on Monday, as losses in the Oil & Gas, Capital Goods and Consumer Durables sectors led shares lower. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 lost 0.49%, while the BSE Sensex 30 index declined 0.29%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd (NSE:PGRD), which rose 3.04% or 8.00 points to trade at 271.30 at the close. Meanwhile,  Hindustan Unilever  Ltd. (NSE:HLL) added 1.96% or 43.20 points to end at 2,247.75 and  Infosys  Ltd (NSE:INFY) was up 0.92% or 15.45 points to 1,701.45 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Oil And Natural Gas Corporation Ltd (NSE:ONGC), which fell 4.17% or 9.70 points to trade at 223.19 at the close.  Trent  Ltd (NSE:TREN) declined 4.00% or 200.15 points to end at 4,799.70 and  IndusInd Bank  Ltd. (NSE:INBK) was down 3.87% or 36.25 points to 900.50. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd (BO:PGRD) which rose 2.85% to 270.70, Hindustan Unilever Ltd. (BO:HLL) which was up 1.97% to settle at 2,247.25 and Infosys Ltd (BO:INFY) which gained 0.85% to close at 1,700.00. The worst performers were IndusInd Bank Ltd. (BO:INBK) which was down 3.84% to 900.85 in late trade, Zomato Ltd (BO:ZOMT) which lost 2.33% to settle at 211.75 and Larsen & Toubro Ltd (BO:LART) which was down 2.05% to 3,179.00 at the close. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 1950 to 416 and 78 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 2761 fell and 1156 advanced, while 144 ended unchanged.
