Investing.com – Australia stocks were lower after the close on Friday, as losses in the A-REITs, IT and Financials sectors led shares lower. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 declined 1.81% to hit a new 6-months low. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Insignia Financial Ltd (ASX:IFL), which rose 9.62% or 0.41 points to trade at 4.67 at the close. Meanwhile, Elders Ltd (ASX:ELD) added 3.17% or 0.22 points to end at 7.15 and Nuix Ltd (ASX:NXL) was up 2.45% or 0.08 points to 3.35 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Polynovo Ltd (ASX:PNV), which fell 12.32% or 0.17 points to trade at 1.21 at the close.  Macquarie Group  Ltd (ASX:MQG) declined 5.18% or 11.42 points to end at 209.08 and  Rea Group Ltd  (ASX:REA) was down 5.09% or 13.07 points to 243.50. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 707 to 340 and 368 ended unchanged. Shares in Insignia Financial Ltd (ASX:IFL) rose to 3-years highs; rising 9.62% or 0.41 to 4.67. Shares in Polynovo Ltd (ASX:PNV) fell to 52-week lows; down 12.32% or 0.17 to 1.21.  The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 9.72% to 14.22 a new 6-months high. Gold Futures for April delivery was down 0.28% or 8.21 to $2,918.39 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April rose 0.09% or 0.06 to hit $66.42 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract rose 0.16% or 0.11 to trade at $69.57 a barrel.
