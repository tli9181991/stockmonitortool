Investing.com – Russia stocks were lower after the close on Monday, as losses in the Oil & Gas, Mining and Telecoms sectors led shares lower. At the close in Moscow, the MOEX Russia Index lost 3.25%. The best performers of the session on the MOEX Russia Index were Polyus PJSC (MCX:PLZL), which rose 2.51% or 42.80 points to trade at 1,744.60 at the close. Meanwhile, Aeroflot PJSC (MCX:AFLT) added 1.22% or 0.82 points to end at 68.04 and Moskovskaya Birzha PJSC (MCX:MOEX) was up 0.76% or 1.47 points to 194.15 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Moskovskiy Kreditnyi Bank PAO (MCX:CBOM), which fell 3.71% or 0.28 points to trade at 7.19 at the close. Gazprom PJSC (MCX:GAZP) declined 2.92% or 4.05 points to end at 134.84 and NOVATEK PJSC (MCX:NVTK) was down 2.86% or 31.80 points to 1,079.00. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Moscow Stock Exchange by 187 to 59 and 9 ended unchanged. The Russian Volatility Index – RVI, which measures the implied volatility of MOEX Russia Index options, was up 0.98% to 48.67. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 3.09% or 100.30 to $3,343.60 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 1.85% or 1.08 to hit $57.21 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 1.48% or 0.91 to trade at $60.38 a barrel.
