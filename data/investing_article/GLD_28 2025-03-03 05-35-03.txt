Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Energy, IT and Resources sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 gained 0.90%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were  Appen Ltd  (ASX:APX), which rose 5.34% or 0.07 points to trade at 1.39 at the close. Meanwhile, St Barbara Ltd (ASX:SBM) added 6.98% or 0.02 points to end at 0.23 and EML Payments Ltd (ASX:EML) was up 7.22% or 0.07 points to 1.05 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  NRW Holdings  Ltd (ASX:NWH), which fell 9.42% or 0.31 points to trade at 2.98 at the close.  TPG Telecom Ltd  (ASX:TPG) declined 2.59% or 0.12 points to end at 4.51 and Suncorp Group Ltd (ASX:SUN) was down 2.78% or 0.56 points to 19.55. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 566 to 526 and 441 ended unchanged. The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 7.15% to 12.70. Gold Futures for April delivery was up 0.79% or 22.50 to $2,871.00 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April rose 0.43% or 0.30 to hit $70.06 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract rose 0.41% or 0.30 to trade at $73.11 a barrel.
