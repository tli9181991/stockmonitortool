Investing.com – Brazil stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Real Estate, Public Utilities and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa gained 0.34% to hit a new all time high. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were JBS SA (BVMF:JBSS3), which rose 4.79% or 1.92 points to trade at 42.01 at the close. Meanwhile, Marfrig Alimentos SA (BVMF:MRFG3) added 4.31% or 1.01 points to end at 24.47 and  Grupo Vamos SA  (BVMF:VAMO3) was up 4.21% or 0.19 points to 4.70 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Cogna Educacao SA (BVMF:COGN3), which fell 7.79% or 0.24 points to trade at 2.84 at the close. YDUQS Participacoes SA (BVMF:YDUQ3) declined 5.07% or 0.80 points to end at 14.97 and Natura & Co SA (BVMF:NTCO3) was down 3.17% or 0.34 points to 10.39. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 548 to 386 and 65 ended unchanged. The CBOE Brazil Etf Volatility, which measures the implied volatility of Bovespa options, was down 9.44% to 26.08 a new 3-months low. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 1.81% or 58.52 to $3,292.02 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July rose 0.03% or 0.02 to hit $62.16 a barrel, while the July US coffee C contract fell 1.53% or 5.75 to trade at $368.95 .
