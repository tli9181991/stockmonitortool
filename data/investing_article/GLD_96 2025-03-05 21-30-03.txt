Investing.com – Brazil stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Industrials, Financials and Consumption sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa added 0.20%. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were Embraer SA (BVMF:EMBR3), which rose 8.79% or 6.13 points to trade at 75.85 at the close. Meanwhile, Marfrig Alimentos SA (BVMF:MRFG3) added 7.04% or 0.96 points to end at 14.59 and Ambev SA (BVMF:ABEV3) was up 4.58% or 0.56 points to 12.78 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Brava Energia SA (BVMF:BRAV3), which fell 8.27% or 1.49 points to trade at 16.52 at the close. Petroleo Brasileiro SA (BVMF:PETR3) declined 4.61% or 1.80 points to end at 37.25 and Automob Participações SA (BVMF:AMOB3) was down 4.00% or 0.01 points to 0.24. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 565 to 373 and 55 ended unchanged. Shares in Embraer SA (BVMF:EMBR3) rose to all time highs; up 8.79% or 6.13 to 75.85. Shares in Automob Participações SA (BVMF:AMOB3) fell to all time lows; losing 4.00% or 0.01 to 0.24.  The CBOE Brazil Etf Volatility, which measures the implied volatility of Bovespa options, was down 1.70% to 30.09. Gold Futures for April delivery was up 0.29% or 8.59 to $2,929.19 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April fell 2.75% or 1.88 to hit $66.38 a barrel, while the May US coffee C contract rose 2.36% or 9.40 to trade at $407.80 . USD/BRL was down 2.28% to 5.75, while EUR/BRL fell 6.45% to 6.20. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 1.35% at 104.26.
