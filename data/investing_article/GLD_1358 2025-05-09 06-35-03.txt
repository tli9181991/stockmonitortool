Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the IT, Financials and Consumer Staples sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 added 0.48%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Nuix Ltd (ASX:NXL), which rose 10.19% or 0.21 points to trade at 2.27 at the close. Meanwhile, Nine Entertainment Co Holdings Ltd (ASX:NEC) added 6.04% or 0.09 points to end at 1.58 and Super Retail Group Ltd (ASX:SUL) was up 4.82% or 0.65 points to 14.14 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Unibail Rodamco Westfield  (ASX:URW), which fell 5.46% or 0.37 points to trade at 6.41 at the close. Lynas Rare Earths Ltd (ASX:LYC) declined 3.39% or 0.27 points to end at 7.70 and Cromwell Property Group (ASX:CMW) was down 2.56% or 0.01 points to 0.38. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 582 to 497 and 462 ended unchanged. The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 9.31% to 13.64. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.68% or 22.46 to $3,328.46 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June rose 0.60% or 0.36 to hit $60.27 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract rose 0.60% or 0.38 to trade at $63.22 a barrel. AUD/USD was unchanged 0.05% to 0.64, while AUD/JPY fell 0.32% to 93.09. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.12% at 100.36.
