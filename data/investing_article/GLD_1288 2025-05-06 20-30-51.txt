Investing.com – Canada stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Materials, Energy and Telecoms sectors led shares higher. At the close in Toronto, the S&P/TSX Composite added 0.08%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/TSX Composite were  Centerra Gold Inc  (TSX:CG), which rose 15.68% or 1.40 points to trade at 10.33 at the close. Meanwhile,  Ero Copper  Corp (TSX:ERO) added 11.01% or 1.93 points to end at 19.46 and  Orla Mining Ltd  (TSX:OLA) was up 9.58% or 1.42 points to 16.24 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Shopify Inc (TSX:SHOP), which fell 4.87% or 6.61 points to trade at 129.24 at the close. Bausch Health Companies Inc (TSX:BHC) declined 3.96% or 0.27 points to end at 6.55 and CES Energy Solutions Corp (TSX:CEU) was down 3.38% or 0.21 points to 6.01. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Toronto Stock Exchange by 499 to 379 and 80 ended unchanged. Shares in Orla Mining Ltd (TSX:OLA) rose to all time highs; rising 9.58% or 1.42 to 16.24.  The S&P/TSX 60 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/TSX Composite options, was up 1.14% to 18.68. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 3.33% or 110.70 to $3,433.00 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June rose 3.31% or 1.89 to hit $59.02 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract rose 3.02% or 1.82 to trade at $62.05 a barrel.
