Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Energy, IT and Industrials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 gained 1.33%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were  Appen Ltd  (ASX:APX), which rose 8.97% or 0.07 points to trade at 0.85 at the close. Meanwhile, Polynovo Ltd (ASX:PNV) added 8.13% or 0.09 points to end at 1.13 and Seek Ltd (ASX:SEK) was up 6.24% or 1.23 points to 20.95 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Ramelius Resources  Ltd (ASX:RMS), which fell 14.97% or 0.44 points to trade at 2.50 at the close. St Barbara Ltd (ASX:SBM) declined 11.29% or 0.04 points to end at 0.28 and  Evolution Mining  Ltd (ASX:EVN) was down 10.94% or 0.97 points to 7.90. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 668 to 447 and 390 ended unchanged. The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was down 5.88% to 13.17. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 2.53% or 86.61 to $3,332.79 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June rose 1.59% or 1.01 to hit $64.68 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract rose 1.51% or 1.02 to trade at $68.46 a barrel.
