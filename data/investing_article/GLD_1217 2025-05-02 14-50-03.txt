Investing.com – Greece stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the Banking, Construction and Travel sectors led shares higher. At the close in Athens, the Athens General Composite added 1.65% to hit a new 1-month high. The best performers of the session on the Athens General Composite were  Ekter  (AT:EKTr), which rose 5.50% or 0.11 points to trade at 2.02 at the close. Meanwhile, Piraeus Bank SA (AT:BOPr) added 4.89% or 0.24 points to end at 5.15 and  Alpha Bank  SA (AT:ACBr) was up 4.79% or 0.10 points to 2.23 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Techn Olympic  (AT:OLYr), which fell 1.79% or 0.04 points to trade at 2.20 at the close. Titan Cement International SA (AT:TITCr) declined 1.60% or 0.65 points to end at 40.10 and  Alumil  (AT:ALMr) was down 1.57% or 0.08 points to 5.00. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Athens Stock Exchange by 84 to 44 and 18 ended unchanged. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 1.33% or 43.01 to $3,265.21 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 1.60% or 0.95 to hit $58.29 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 1.42% or 0.88 to trade at $61.25 a barrel. EUR/USD was up 0.66% to 1.14, while EUR/GBP unchanged 0.32% to 0.85. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.74% at 99.31.
