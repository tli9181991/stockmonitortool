Investing.com – India stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Technology, Oil & Gas and Auto sectors led shares higher. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 added 1.24% to hit a new 1-month high, while the BSE Sensex 30 index gained 1.19%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  Bharti Airtel  Ltd. (NSE:BRTI), which rose 4.16% or 68.10 points to trade at 1,705.25 at the close. Meanwhile, Titan Company Ltd (NSE:TITN) added 3.80% or 116.80 points to end at 3,193.10 and  Britannia Industries  Ltd (NSE:BRIT) was up 2.63% or 124.00 points to 4,831.10 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  IndusInd Bank  Ltd. (NSE:INBK), which fell 1.24% or 8.60 points to trade at 684.10 at the close.  Bajaj Finance  Ltd (NSE:BJFN) declined 0.59% or 51.95 points to end at 8,679.65 and  Trent  Ltd (NSE:TREN) was down 0.18% or 9.30 points to 5,221.55. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were Bharti Airtel Ltd (BO:BRTI) which rose 4.05% to 1,703.00, Titan Company Ltd (BO:TITN) which was up 3.47% to settle at 3,181.95 and Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd. (BO:MAHM) which gained 1.79% to close at 2,836.15. The worst performers were IndusInd Bank Ltd. (BO:INBK) which was down 1.03% to 685.25 in late trade, Bajaj Finance Ltd (BO:BJFN) which lost 0.57% to settle at 8,687.15 and UltraTech Cement Ltd (BO:ULTC) which was down 0.15% to 10,917.80 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 1378 to 955 and 104 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 2265 rose and 1589 declined, while 128 ended unchanged. The India VIX, which measures the implied volatility of Nifty 50 options, was down 5.75% to 12.53 a new 3-months low. Gold Futures for April delivery was down 0.18% or 5.55 to $3,035.65 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 0.10% or 0.07 to hit $66.84 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract fell 0.10% or 0.07 to trade at $70.71 a barrel.
