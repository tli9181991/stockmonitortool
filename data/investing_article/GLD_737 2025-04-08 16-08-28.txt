Investing.com – Italy stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Industrials, Travel & Leisure and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Milan, the Investing.com Italy 40 gained 2.58%. The best performers of the session on the Investing.com Italy 40 were Leonardo SpA (BIT:LDOF), which rose 7.45% or 2.91 points to trade at 41.95 at the close. Meanwhile,  Unipol  Gruppo Finanziario Spa (BIT:UNPI) added 7.25% or 0.90 points to end at 13.31 and Banca  Mediolanum  SpA (BIT:BMED) was up 5.37% or 0.65 points to 12.76 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Eni SpA (BIT:ENI), which fell 1.28% or 0.15 points to trade at 11.89 at the close. Stellantis NV (BIT:STLAM) declined 0.84% or 0.07 points to end at 8.22 and  Tenaris  SA (BIT:TENR) was up 0.45% or 0.07 points to 14.62. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Milan Stock Exchange by 481 to 85 and 31 ended unchanged. Shares in Eni SpA (BIT:ENI) fell to 52-week lows; down 1.28% or 0.15 to 11.89. Shares in Stellantis NV (BIT:STLAM) fell to 3-years lows; losing 0.84% or 0.07 to 8.22.  Crude oil for May delivery was up 0.86% or 0.52 to $61.22 a barrel. EUR/USD was unchanged 0.45% to 1.09, while EUR/GBP rose 0.59% to 0.85. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.09% at 103.06.
