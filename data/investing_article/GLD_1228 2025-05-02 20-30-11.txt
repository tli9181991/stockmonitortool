Investing.com – Brazil stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the Electric Power, Real Estate and Basic Materials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa gained 0.05% to hit a new 6-months high. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were Prio SA (BVMF:PRIO3), which rose 8.07% or 2.72 points to trade at 36.43 at the close. Meanwhile, YDUQS Participacoes SA (BVMF:YDUQ3) added 4.90% or 0.70 points to end at 14.99 and Automob Participações SA (BVMF:AMOB3) was up 3.70% or 0.01 points to 0.28 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Companhia Brasileira De Distribuica  (BVMF:PCAR3), which fell 7.80% or 0.33 points to trade at 3.90 at the close. Hypera SA (BVMF:HYPE3) declined 4.77% or 1.15 points to end at 22.97 and  Pet Center Comercio e Partcipacoes  (BVMF:PETZ3) was down 4.09% or 0.19 points to 4.46. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 580 to 391 and 41 ended unchanged. The CBOE Brazil Etf Volatility, which measures the implied volatility of Bovespa options, was up 1.07% to 30.10. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.74% or 23.74 to $3,245.94 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 1.25% or 0.74 to hit $58.50 a barrel, while the July US coffee C contract fell 0.53% or 2.05 to trade at $382.60 . USD/BRL was down 0.37% to 5.65, while EUR/BRL fell 0.32% to 6.39. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.20% at 99.85.
