Investing.com – Norway stocks were lower after the close on Monday, as losses in the Media, Transport and Diversified Financials sectors led shares lower. At the close in Oslo, the Oslo OBX lost 0.12%. The best performers of the session on the Oslo OBX were  Golden Ocean  Group Ltd (OL:GOGL), which rose 3.70% or 2.98 points to trade at 83.56 at the close. Meanwhile,  Gjensidige Forsikring ASA  (OL:GJFG) added 1.65% or 3.80 points to end at 234.00 and  Hoegh Autoliners  ASA (OL:HAUTO) was up 1.42% or 1.15 points to 82.10 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Tomra Systems  ASA (OL:TOM), which fell 2.28% or 3.70 points to trade at 158.50 at the close.  Var Energi  ASA (OL:VAR) declined 1.72% or 0.57 points to end at 32.65 and  Hafnia Ltd  (OL:HAFNI) was down 1.57% or 0.74 points to 46.26. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Oslo Stock Exchange by 165 to 113 and 22 ended unchanged. Crude oil for May delivery was up 1.33% or 0.91 to $69.19 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in June rose 1.16% or 0.83 to hit $72.44 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract fell 0.03% or 0.99 to trade at $3,047.41 a troy ounce. EUR/NOK was unchanged 0.01% to 11.36, while USD/NOK rose 0.18% to 10.53. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.28% at 104.04.
