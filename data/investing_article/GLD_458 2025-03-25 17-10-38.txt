Investing.com – Sweden stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Financials, Technology and Consumer Services sectors led shares higher. At the close in Stockholm, the OMX Stockholm 30 added 0.40%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Stockholm 30 were  Swedbank  AB ser A (ST:SWEDa), which rose 1.82% or 4.70 points to trade at 263.30 at the close. Meanwhile, Electrolux AB Class B (ST:ELUXb) added 1.62% or 1.40 points to end at 88.90 and Atlas Copco AB Class A (ST:ATCOa) was up 1.40% or 2.40 points to 169.90 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were NIBE Industrier AB ser. B (ST:NIBEb), which fell 4.84% or 2.10 points to trade at 40.90 at the close. Samhallsbyggnadsbolaget i Norden AB (ST:SBBb) declined 1.86% or 0.07 points to end at 3.49 and AstraZeneca PLC (ST:AZN) was down 1.66% or 25.00 points to 1,477.50. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Stockholm Stock Exchange by 418 to 324 and 85 ended unchanged. Crude oil for May delivery was down 0.35% or 0.24 to $68.87 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in June fell 0.21% or 0.15 to hit $72.22 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 0.42% or 12.89 to trade at $3,056.89 a troy ounce. EUR/SEK was down 0.67% to 10.82, while USD/SEK fell 0.80% to 10.01. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.22% at 103.71.
