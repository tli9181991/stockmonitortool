Investing.com – Finland stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Telecoms, Financials and Consumer Services sectors led shares higher. At the close in Helsinki, the OMX Helsinki 25 gained 1.25%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Helsinki 25 were Nokia Oyj (HE:NOKIA), which rose 5.62% or 0.26 points to trade at 4.89 at the close. Meanwhile, KONE Oyj (HE:KNEBV) added 2.92% or 1.58 points to end at 55.78 and Outokumpu Oyj (HE:OUT1V) was up 2.83% or 0.10 points to 3.67 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Wartsila Oyj Abp (HE:WRT1V), which fell 2.13% or 0.39 points to trade at 17.89 at the close.  Kojamo  (HE:KOJAMO) declined 1.30% or 0.12 points to end at 9.12 and  Fortum  Oyj (HE:FORTUM) was down 1.23% or 0.19 points to 14.91. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Helsinki Stock Exchange by 113 to 59 and 17 ended unchanged. Shares in Nokia Oyj (HE:NOKIA) rose to 52-week highs; gaining 5.62% or 0.26 to 4.89. Shares in KONE Oyj (HE:KNEBV) rose to 3-years highs; gaining 2.92% or 1.58 to 55.78.  Brent oil for May delivery was down 0.23% or 0.17 to $72.64 a barrel.
