Investing.com – Greece stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Construction, Banking and Travel sectors led shares higher. At the close in Athens, the Athens General Composite rose 0.77% to hit a new 5-year high. The best performers of the session on the Athens General Composite were  Ellaktor  SA (AT:HELr), which rose 4.60% or 0.06 points to trade at 1.32 at the close. Meanwhile,  Ekter  (AT:EKTr) added 4.50% or 0.09 points to end at 2.09 and  Techn Olympic  (AT:OLYr) was up 3.57% or 0.08 points to 2.32 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Iktinos Hellas  (AT:IKTr), which fell 2.32% or 0.01 points to trade at 0.34 at the close.  Jumbo  SA (AT:BABr) declined 1.59% or 0.46 points to end at 28.44 and  Alpha Bank  SA (AT:ACBr) was down 1.18% or 0.03 points to 2.52. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Athens Stock Exchange by 78 to 47 and 21 ended unchanged. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.66% or 21.15 to $3,249.15 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June rose 1.61% or 1.00 to hit $62.95 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract rose 1.40% or 0.91 to trade at $65.87 a barrel. EUR/USD was up 0.62% to 1.12, while EUR/GBP unchanged 0.06% to 0.84. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.51% at 101.08.
