Investing.com – Turkey stocks were lower after the close on Wednesday, as losses in the Leasing & Factoring, Non-Metal Mineral Products and Transport sectors led shares lower. At the close in Istanbul, the BIST 100 fell 1.41%. The best performers of the session on the BIST 100 were  Katilimevim Tasarruf Finansman AS  (IS:KTLEV), which rose 9.98% or 6.45 points to trade at 71.10 at the close. Meanwhile, Grainturk Holding AS (IS:GRTHO) added 5.98% or 19.75 points to end at 350.00 and Borusan Yatirim ve Pazarlama AS (IS:BRYAT) was up 5.13% or 99.00 points to 2,030.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Tekfen Holding  AS (IS:TKFEN), which fell 10.00% or 15.00 points to trade at 135.00 at the close.  Cimsa  Cimento Sanayi ve Ticaret AS (IS:CIMSA) declined 6.94% or 3.53 points to end at 47.32 and Is Yatirim Menkul Degerler AS (IS:ISMEN) was down 5.27% or 2.12 points to 38.14. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Istanbul Stock Exchange by 318 to 257 and 16 ended unchanged. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.64% or 20.21 to $3,166.21 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May rose 0.37% or 0.26 to hit $71.46 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract rose 0.16% or 0.12 to trade at $74.61 a barrel. USD/TRY was down 0.04% to 37.92, while EUR/TRY rose 0.63% to 41.20. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.42% at 103.51.
