Russia stocks lower at close of trade; MOEX Russia Index down 0.41%
Investing.com – Russia stocks were lower after the close on Wednesday, as losses in the Mining, Power and Telecoms sectors led shares lower. At the close in Moscow, the MOEX Russia Index declined 0.41%. The best performers of the session on the MOEX Russia Index were  PhosAgro  PJSC (MCX:PHOR), which rose 0.70% or 46.00 points to trade at 6,576.00 at the close. Meanwhile, LUKOIL PJSC (MCX:LKOH) added 0.24% or 16.00 points to end at 6,600.00 and NOVATEK PJSC (MCX:NVTK) was up 0.23% or 2.60 points to 1,143.60 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Moskovskiy Kreditnyi Bank PAO (MCX:CBOM), which fell 5.21% or 0.40 points to trade at 7.27 at the close. PIK SHb PJSC (MCX:PIKK) declined 4.06% or 19.50 points to end at 461.00 and OK Rusal MKPAO (MCX:RUAL) was down 3.15% or 1.03 points to 31.72. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Moscow Stock Exchange by 177 to 65 and 10 ended unchanged. The Russian Volatility Index – RVI, which measures the implied volatility of MOEX Russia Index options, was up 1.18% to 41.14. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.98% or 32.15 to $3,316.75 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July fell 1.10% or 0.68 to hit $61.35 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 0.95% or 0.62 to trade at $64.76 a barrel. USD/RUB was unchanged 0.00% to 79.74, while EUR/RUB unchanged 0.00% to 90.35. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.50% at 99.48.
