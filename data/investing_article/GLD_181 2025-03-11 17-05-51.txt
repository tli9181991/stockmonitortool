Investing.com – France stocks were lower after the close on Tuesday, as losses in the Technology, Basic Materials and Healthcare sectors led shares lower. At the close in Paris, the CAC 40 lost 1.31% to hit a new 1-month low, while the SBF 120 index lost 1.30%. The best performers of the session on the CAC 40 were Schneider Electric SE (EPA:SCHN), which rose 1.67% or 3.65 points to trade at 222.75 at the close. Meanwhile,  Renault  SA (EPA:RENA) added 1.12% or 0.53 points to end at 47.73 and Unibail-Rodamco-Westfield SE (EPA:URW) was up 0.43% or 0.32 points to 74.86 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Stellantis NV (EPA:STLAM), which fell 5.30% or 0.63 points to trade at 11.32 at the close.  Accor  SA (EPA:ACCP) declined 4.25% or 1.95 points to end at 43.98 and Capgemini SE (EPA:CAPP) was down 3.83% or 5.85 points to 146.95. The worst performers were Atos SE (EPA:ATOS) which was unchanged 13.95% to 0.00 in late trade, Air France KLM SA (EPA:AIRF) which lost 9.27% to settle at 10.37 and  Valeo  SA (EPA:VLOF) which was down 6.40% to 10.02 at the close. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Paris Stock Exchange by 349 to 138 and 82 ended unchanged. The CAC 40 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of CAC 40 options, was unchanged 0.00% to 18.96 a new 52-week high.
