Investing.com – Brazil stocks were lower after the close on Monday, as losses in the Industrials, Consumption and Public Utilities sectors led shares lower. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa lost 0.77%. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were Brava Energia SA (BVMF:BRAV3), which rose 10.19% or 1.97 points to trade at 21.30 at the close. Meanwhile,  Cvc Brasil ON  (BVMF:CVCB3) added 7.07% or 0.14 points to end at 2.12 and Usinas Siderurgicas de Minas Gerais SA USIMINAS Pref (BVMF:USIM5) was up 1.38% or 0.08 points to 5.87 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Embraer SA (BVMF:EMBR3), which fell 4.70% or 3.51 points to trade at 71.25 at the close. Hapvida Participacoes e Investimentos (BVMF:HAPV3) declined 4.15% or 0.09 points to end at 2.08 and RUMO Logistica Operadora Multimodal SA (BVMF:RAIL3) was down 3.94% or 0.70 points to 17.08. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 538 to 411 and 57 ended unchanged. The CBOE Brazil Etf Volatility, which measures the implied volatility of Bovespa options, was down 0.33% to 30.22. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 0.09% or 2.71 to $3,045.69 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May rose 1.33% or 0.91 to hit $69.19 a barrel, while the May US coffee C contract rose 0.66% or 2.60 to trade at $394.00 .
