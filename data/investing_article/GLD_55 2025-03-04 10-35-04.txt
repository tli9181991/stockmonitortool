Investing.com – India stocks were lower after the close on Tuesday, as losses in the Auto, Technology and Fast Moving Consumer Goods sectors led shares lower. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 declined 0.18% to hit a new 6-months low, while the BSE Sensex 30 index lost 0.13%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  Bharat Petroleum  Corp. Ltd. (NSE:BPCL), which rose 3.10% or 7.51 points to trade at 249.92 at the close. Meanwhile, State Bank Of India (NSE:SBI) added 2.98% or 20.75 points to end at 716.05 and  Bharat Electronics  Ltd (NSE:BAJE) was up 2.84% or 7.32 points to 264.71 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Bajaj Auto  Ltd (NSE:BAJA), which fell 4.95% or 381.50 points to trade at 7,333.30 at the close.  Hero MotoCorp  Ltd (NSE:HROM) declined 3.19% or 115.90 points to end at 3,514.85 and Bajaj Finserv Ltd (NSE:BJFS) was down 2.62% or 48.25 points to 1,790.30. (BO:TCS) which gained 1.04% to close at 3,533.00. The worst performers were Bajaj Finserv Ltd (BO:BJFS) which was down 2.70% to 1,788.60 in late trade, HCL Technologies Ltd (BO:HCLT) which lost 2.40% to settle at 1,535.20 and Nestle India Ltd (BO:NEST) which was down 1.82% to 2,145.95 at the close.
