India stocks lower at close of trade; Nifty 50 down 0.82%
Investing.com – India stocks were lower after the close on Thursday, as losses in the Oil & Gas, Consumer Durables and Auto sectors led shares lower. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 fell 0.82%, while the BSE Sensex 30 index declined 0.79%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  IndusInd Bank  Ltd. (NSE:INBK), which rose 1.81% or 13.90 points to trade at 783.85 at the close. Meanwhile,  JSW Steel  Ltd (NSE:JSTL) added 0.71% or 7.10 points to end at 1,008.00 and  Bajaj Auto  Ltd (NSE:BAJA) was up 0.62% or 53.50 points to 8,741.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Oil And Natural Gas Corporation Ltd (NSE:ONGC), which fell 2.65% or 6.58 points to trade at 242.10 at the close.  Mahindra & Mahindra  Ltd. (NSE:MAHM) declined 2.41% or 74.60 points to end at 3,016.10 and  Hindalco Industries  Ltd. (NSE:HALC) was down 2.03% or 13.45 points to 649.55. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were IndusInd Bank Ltd. (BO:INBK) which rose 1.73% to 784.45, Bharti Airtel Ltd (BO:BRTI) which was up 0.44% to settle at 1,830.35 and UltraTech Cement Ltd (BO:ULTC) which gained 0.10% to close at 11,671.00. The worst performers were Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd. (BO:MAHM) which was down 2.27% to 3,017.70 in late trade, Tech Mahindra Ltd (BO:TEML) which lost 1.77% to settle at 1,569.85 and ITC Ltd (BO:ITC) which was down 1.72% to 425.50 at the close. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 1312 to 1047 and 49 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 2028 fell and 1731 advanced, while 161 ended unchanged. The India VIX, which measures the implied volatility of Nifty 50 options, was down 1.99% to 17.20. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 0.54% or 17.81 to $3,295.69 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July fell 1.61% or 0.99 to hit $60.58 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 1.60% or 1.04 to trade at $63.87 a barrel. USD/INR was up 0.40% to 85.93, while EUR/INR fell 0.03% to 97.13. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.30% at 99.74.
