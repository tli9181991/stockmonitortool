Investing.com – Brazil stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the Consumption, Industrials and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa gained 0.12% to hit a new 6-months high. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were Marfrig Alimentos SA (BVMF:MRFG3), which rose 8.45% or 1.78 points to trade at 22.85 at the close. Meanwhile, Minerva SA/Brazil (BVMF:BEEF3) added 4.40% or 0.33 points to end at 7.83 and  Auren Energia SA  (BVMF:AURE3) was up 4.07% or 0.35 points to 8.87 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Azul SA Pref  (BVMF:AZUL4), which fell 17.37% or 0.41 points to trade at 1.95 at the close. Cyrela Brazil Realty SA (BVMF:CYRE3) declined 5.27% or 1.42 points to end at 25.53 and YDUQS Participacoes SA (BVMF:YDUQ3) was down 4.13% or 0.61 points to 14.16. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 547 to 386 and 43 ended unchanged. Shares in Marfrig Alimentos SA (BVMF:MRFG3) rose to 3-years highs; gaining 8.45% or 1.78 to 22.85. Shares in Azul SA Pref (BVMF:AZUL4) fell to all time lows; down 17.37% or 0.41 to 1.95.  The CBOE Brazil Etf Volatility, which measures the implied volatility of Bovespa options, was down 5.93% to 32.68. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 0.85% or 28.34 to $3,320.26 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June rose 0.76% or 0.48 to hit $63.27 a barrel, while the July US coffee C contract rose 0.05% or 0.20 to trade at $399.00 .
