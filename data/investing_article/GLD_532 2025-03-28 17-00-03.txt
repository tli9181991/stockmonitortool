Investing.com – Finland stocks were lower after the close on Friday, as losses in the Industrials, Oil & Gas and Technology sectors led shares lower. At the close in Helsinki, the OMX Helsinki 25 fell 0.94% to hit a new 1-month low. The best performers of the session on the OMX Helsinki 25 were Orion Oyj B (HE:ORNBV), which rose 2.39% or 1.28 points to trade at 54.78 at the close. Meanwhile,  Stora Enso  Oyj R (HE:STERV) added 2.38% or 0.21 points to end at 9.14 and  Kojamo  (HE:KOJAMO) was up 1.73% or 0.14 points to 8.55 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Konecranes  ABP (HE:KCRA), which fell 12.89% or 8.95 points to trade at 60.50 at the close.  Kalmar Oyj  (HE:KALMAR) declined 5.59% or 1.87 points to end at 31.52 and Metso Oyj (HE:METSO) was down 4.14% or 0.43 points to 9.85. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Helsinki Stock Exchange by 101 to 74 and 12 ended unchanged. Brent oil for June delivery was down 0.94% or 0.69 to $72.65 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 0.92% or 0.64 to hit $69.28 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 0.88% or 27.09 to trade at $3,117.99 a troy ounce. EUR/USD was unchanged 0.19% to 1.08, while EUR/GBP unchanged 0.25% to 0.84. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.27% at 103.71.
