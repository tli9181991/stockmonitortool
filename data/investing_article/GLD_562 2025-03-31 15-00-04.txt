Investing.com – Norway stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Healthcare Equipment & Services, Pharma Biotech & Life Sciences and Utilities sectors led shares higher. At the close in Oslo, the Oslo OBX added 0.45%. The best performers of the session on the Oslo OBX were  Var Energi  ASA (OL:VAR), which rose 2.35% or 0.78 points to trade at 33.91 at the close. Meanwhile, Aker BP ASA (OL:AKRBP) added 2.25% or 5.50 points to end at 249.50 and SalMar ASA (OL:SALM) was up 2.02% or 10.00 points to 504.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Golden Ocean  Group Ltd (OL:GOGL), which fell 3.73% or 3.26 points to trade at 84.22 at the close.  Tomra Systems  ASA (OL:TOM) declined 2.74% or 4.20 points to end at 149.10 and Norsk Hydro ASA (OL:NHY) was down 2.17% or 1.34 points to 60.32. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Oslo Stock Exchange by 203 to 83 and 18 ended unchanged. Crude oil for May delivery was up 2.34% or 1.62 to $70.98 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in June rose 1.98% or 1.44 to hit $74.20 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 1.35% or 42.11 to trade at $3,156.41 a troy ounce. EUR/NOK was up 0.53% to 11.38, while USD/NOK rose 1.05% to 10.53. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.20% at 103.91.
