Investing.com – France stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Technology, Industrials and Consumer Goods sectors led shares higher. At the close in Paris, the CAC 40 rose 2.13%, while the SBF 120 index gained 2.05%. The best performers of the session on the CAC 40 were Eurofins Scientific SE (EPA:EUFI), which rose 12.02% or 5.93 points to trade at 55.28 at the close. Meanwhile, STMicroelectronics NV (EPA:STMPA) added 5.95% or 1.08 points to end at 19.23 and ArcelorMittal SA (AS:MT) was up 5.34% or 1.29 points to 25.47 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Thales  (EPA:TCFP), which fell 0.83% or 2.10 points to trade at 250.80 at the close. Orange SA (EPA:ORAN) declined 0.71% or 0.09 points to end at 12.66 and Engie SA (EPA:ENGIE) was down 0.66% or 0.13 points to 18.77. The top performers on the SBF 120 were Eurofins Scientific SE (EPA:EUFI) which rose 12.02% to 55.28, Opmobility SE (EPA:OPM) which was up 8.45% to settle at 9.44 and  Ubisoft  Entertainment (EPA:UBIP) which gained 6.26% to close at 10.20. The worst performers were SES (EPA:SESFd) which was down 7.96% to 4.55 in late trade, Eutelsat Communications SA (EPA:ETL) which lost 3.22% to settle at 3.61 and Metropole Television SA (EPA:MMTP) which was down 1.43% to 13.76 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Paris Stock Exchange by 331 to 151 and 81 ended unchanged. The CAC 40 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of CAC 40 options, was unchanged 0.00% to 18.96 a new 52-week high. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 3.55% or 121.40 to $3,298.00 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 2.72% or 1.73 to hit $61.94 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract fell 2.46% or 1.66 to trade at $65.78 a barrel.
