Investing.com – France stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Utilities, Consumer Services and Healthcare sectors led shares higher. At the close in Paris, the CAC 40 rose 0.75% to hit a new 1-month high, while the SBF 120 index climbed 0.73%. The best performers of the session on the CAC 40 were  Kering  SA (EPA:PRTP), which rose 3.97% or 6.98 points to trade at 182.60 at the close. Meanwhile, Orange SA (EPA:ORAN) added 3.83% or 0.49 points to end at 13.28 and Compagnie Generale des Etablissements  Michelin  SCA (EPA:MICP) was up 2.29% or 0.79 points to 35.30 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Hermes International  SCA (EPA:HRMS), which fell 0.35% or 9.00 points to trade at 2,550.00 at the close.  Accor  SA (EPA:ACCP) declined 0.27% or 0.13 points to end at 47.40 and AXA SA (EPA:AXAF) was down 0.14% or 0.06 points to 41.61. The top performers on the SBF 120 were  Worldline SA  (EPA:WLN) which rose 8.44% to 5.11,  Voltalia SA  (EPA:VLTSA) which was up 4.93% to settle at 8.10 and Emeis SA (EPA:EMEIS) which gained 4.68% to close at 11.40. The worst performers were  Nexity  (EPA:NEXI) which was down 4.09% to 9.49 in late trade, Argan (EPA:ARGAN) which lost 1.74% to settle at 62.30 and X Fab Silicon Foundries EV (EPA:XFAB) which was down 1.60% to 5.22 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Paris Stock Exchange by 288 to 186 and 96 ended unchanged. Shares in Orange SA (EPA:ORAN) rose to 5-year highs; up 3.83% or 0.49 to 13.28.  The CAC 40 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of CAC 40 options, was unchanged 0.00% to 18.96 a new 52-week high. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 1.49% or 48.11 to $3,281.61 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July fell 0.76% or 0.47 to hit $61.67 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 0.78% or 0.51 to trade at $65.03 a barrel.
