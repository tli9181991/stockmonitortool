Investing.com – Norway stocks were lower after the close on Tuesday, as losses in the Media, Transport and Diversified Financials sectors led shares lower. At the close in Oslo, the Oslo OBX fell 0.51%. The best performers of the session on the Oslo OBX were TGS NOPEC Geophysical Company ASA (OL:TGS), which rose 4.45% or 3.35 points to trade at 78.55 at the close. Meanwhile,  Hafnia Ltd  (OL:HAFNI) added 3.13% or 1.35 points to end at 44.47 and  Storebrand  ASA (OL:STB) was up 1.77% or 2.10 points to 120.70 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Hoegh Autoliners  ASA (OL:HAUTO), which fell 5.57% or 4.19 points to trade at 71.07 at the close.  Tomra Systems  ASA (OL:TOM) declined 3.79% or 5.70 points to end at 144.80 and Nel ASA (OL:NEL) was down 3.68% or 0.08 points to 2.20. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Oslo Stock Exchange by 142 to 124 and 34 ended unchanged. Crude oil for June delivery was up 1.41% or 0.88 to $63.29 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in June rose 1.10% or 0.73 to hit $66.99 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 0.51% or 17.54 to trade at $3,442.84 a troy ounce. EUR/NOK was down 0.38% to 11.87, while USD/NOK fell 0.08% to 10.35. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.25% at 98.29.
