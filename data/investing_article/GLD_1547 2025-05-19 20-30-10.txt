Investing.com – Brazil stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Electric Power, Public Utilities and Consumption sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa rose 0.32% to hit a new all time high. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were JBS SA (BVMF:JBSS3), which rose 3.06% or 1.19 points to trade at 40.09 at the close. Meanwhile, Embraer SA (BVMF:EMBR3) added 2.76% or 1.90 points to end at 70.65 and Lojas Renner SA (BVMF:LREN3) was up 2.62% or 0.46 points to 17.99 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Marfrig Alimentos SA (BVMF:MRFG3), which fell 6.42% or 1.61 points to trade at 23.46 at the close.  Companhia Brasileira De Distribuica  (BVMF:PCAR3) declined 3.90% or 0.13 points to end at 3.20 and Automob Participações SA (BVMF:AMOB3) was down 3.70% or 0.01 points to 0.26. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 469 to 454 and 73 ended unchanged. Shares in Lojas Renner SA (BVMF:LREN3) rose to 52-week highs; up 2.62% or 0.46 to 17.99.  The CBOE Brazil Etf Volatility, which measures the implied volatility of Bovespa options, was down 3.71% to 28.80. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 1.40% or 44.47 to $3,231.77 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July rose 0.24% or 0.15 to hit $62.12 a barrel, while the July US coffee C contract rose 2.61% or 9.55 to trade at $375.20 . USD/BRL was down 0.25% to 5.65, while EUR/BRL rose 0.43% to 6.35. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.69% at 100.25.
