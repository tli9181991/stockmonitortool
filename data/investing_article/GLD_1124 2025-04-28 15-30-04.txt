Investing.com – Denmark stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Consumer Goods, Healthcare and Personal & Household Goods sectors led shares higher. At the close in Copenhagen, the OMX Copenhagen 20 rose 1.59%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Copenhagen 20 were  Zealand Pharma  A/S (CSE:ZELA), which rose 2.93% or 12.30 points to trade at 432.80 at the close. Meanwhile,  Novo Nordisk  A/S Class B (CSE:NOVOb) added 2.68% or 10.80 points to end at 414.30 and Rockwool AS B (CSE:ROCKb) was up 2.51% or 7.00 points to 295.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Oersted AS (CSE:ORSTED), which fell 1.78% or 4.60 points to trade at 253.50 at the close. Novozymes A/S B (CSE:NSISb) declined 0.46% or 1.90 points to end at 411.00 and AP Moeller - Maersk A/S B (CSE:MAERSKb) was down 0.23% or 25.00 points to 10,970.00. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Copenhagen Stock Exchange by 82 to 42 and 15 ended unchanged. Shares in Oersted AS (CSE:ORSTED) fell to 52-week lows; falling 1.78% or 4.60 to 253.50.  Crude oil for June delivery was down 2.00% or 1.26 to $61.76 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in July fell 2.10% or 1.38 to hit $64.42 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 1.25% or 41.29 to trade at $3,339.69 a troy ounce.
