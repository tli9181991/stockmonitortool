Australia stocks higher at close of trade; S&P/ASX 200 up 0.15%
Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the IT, Energy and A-REITs sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 added 0.15%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Perenti Global Ltd (ASX:PRN), which rose 6.35% or 0.10 points to trade at 1.59 at the close. Meanwhile,  Megaport Ltd  (ASX:MP1) added 4.32% or 0.54 points to end at 12.91 and  Austal Ltd  (ASX:ASB) was up 3.54% or 0.18 points to 5.13 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Nufarm Ltd  (ASX:NUF), which fell 5.32% or 0.14 points to trade at 2.49 at the close.  Idp Education Ltd  (ASX:IEL) declined 2.91% or 0.25 points to end at 8.33 and Collins Foods Ltd (ASX:CKF) was down 2.73% or 0.22 points to 7.84. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 575 to 473 and 466 ended unchanged. Shares in Perenti Global Ltd (ASX:PRN) rose to 5-year highs; gaining 6.35% or 0.10 to 1.59. Shares in Nufarm Ltd (ASX:NUF) fell to 5-year lows; down 5.32% or 0.14 to 2.49.  The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was down 2.98% to 11.77. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.94% or 30.89 to $3,325.89 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July fell 0.69% or 0.42 to hit $60.78 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 0.64% or 0.41 to trade at $64.03 a barrel. AUD/USD was unchanged 0.20% to 0.64, while AUD/JPY fell 0.06% to 92.26. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.29% at 99.56.
