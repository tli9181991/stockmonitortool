Investing.com – Australia stocks were lower after the close on Monday, as losses in the Metals & Mining, Resources and Materials sectors led shares lower. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 lost 1.74%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Orora Ltd (ASX:ORA), which rose 4.44% or 0.08 points to trade at 1.88 at the close. Meanwhile, Omni Bridgeway Ltd (ASX:OBL) added 3.64% or 0.05 points to end at 1.43 and  Wisetech Global Ltd  (ASX:WTC) was up 1.47% or 1.18 points to 81.23 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Zip Co Ltd (ASX:ZIP), which fell 7.88% or 0.14 points to trade at 1.61 at the close.  Pilbara Minerals Ltd  (ASX:PLS) declined 7.63% or 0.14 points to end at 1.70 and  Champion Iron Ltd  (ASX:CIA) was down 6.33% or 0.33 points to 4.88. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 792 to 254 and 357 ended unchanged. Shares in Pilbara Minerals Ltd (ASX:PLS) fell to 3-years lows; falling 7.63% or 0.14 to 1.70. Shares in Champion Iron Ltd (ASX:CIA) fell to 52-week lows; losing 6.33% or 0.33 to 4.88.  The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 27.42% to 14.85. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.95% or 29.64 to $3,143.94 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 0.39% or 0.27 to hit $69.09 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract fell 0.34% or 0.25 to trade at $72.51 a barrel.
