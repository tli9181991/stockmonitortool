Investing.com – Canada stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Energy, IT and Healthcare sectors led shares higher. At the close in Toronto, the S&P/TSX Composite rose 0.72%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/TSX Composite were  Transcontinental Inc  (TSX:TCLa), which rose 6.91% or 1.19 points to trade at 18.40 at the close. Meanwhile, CES Energy Solutions Corp (TSX:CEU) added 5.68% or 0.39 points to end at 7.26 and  Bombardier Inc  (TSX:BBDb) was up 5.20% or 4.08 points to 82.52 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Mattr Corp (TSX:MATR), which fell 5.46% or 0.55 points to trade at 9.53 at the close.  Labrador Iron Ore Royalty Corp  (TSX:LIF) declined 4.11% or 1.24 points to end at 28.93 and Bausch Health Companies Inc (TSX:BHC) was down 3.37% or 0.34 points to 9.75. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Toronto Stock Exchange by 594 to 343 and 74 ended unchanged. Shares in Mattr Corp (TSX:MATR) fell to 52-week lows; losing 5.46% or 0.55 to 9.53.  The S&P/TSX 60 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/TSX Composite options, was up 2.84% to 16.30. Gold Futures for April delivery was up 0.70% or 20.59 to $2,941.49 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April rose 2.11% or 1.40 to hit $67.65 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract rose 1.97% or 1.37 to trade at $70.93 a barrel. CAD/USD was unchanged 0.51% to 0.70, while CAD/EUR unchanged 0.76% to 0.64. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.27% at 103.55.
