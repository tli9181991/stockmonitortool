Investing.com – Denmark stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Consumer Goods, Technology and Healthcare sectors led shares higher. At the close in Copenhagen, the OMX Copenhagen 20 gained 1.17%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Copenhagen 20 were DSV Panalpina A/S (CSE:DSV), which rose 2.14% or 28.50 points to trade at 1,361.00 at the close. Meanwhile,  Novo Nordisk  A/S Class B (CSE:NOVOb) added 1.96% or 9.20 points to end at 479.00 and NKT Holding (CSE:NKT) was up 1.45% or 6.80 points to 474.40 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Zealand Pharma  A/S (CSE:ZELA), which fell 9.90% or 51.20 points to trade at 465.80 at the close. Tryg A/S (CSE:TRYG) declined 2.68% or 4.40 points to end at 159.70 and  Genmab  (CSE:GMAB) was down 2.54% or 34.00 points to 1,306.00. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Copenhagen Stock Exchange by 82 to 47 and 22 ended unchanged. Shares in Zealand Pharma A/S (CSE:ZELA) fell to 52-week lows; losing 9.90% or 51.20 to 465.80. Shares in Genmab (CSE:GMAB) fell to 5-year lows; falling 2.54% or 34.00 to 1,306.00.
