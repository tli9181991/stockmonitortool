Investing.com – India stocks were lower after the close on Saturday, as losses in the Technology, Auto and Public Sector Undertakings sectors led shares lower. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 declined 1.86% to hit a new 6-months low, while the BSE Sensex 30 index fell 1.90%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  HDFC Bank Ltd  (NSE:HDBK), which rose 1.86% or 31.70 points to trade at 1,732.40 at the close. Meanwhile, Shriram Finance Ltd. (NSE:SHMF) added 1.73% or 10.50 points to end at 617.30 and  Coal India  Ltd (NSE:COAL) was up 1.51% or 5.50 points to 369.35 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Tech Mahindra  Ltd (NSE:TEML), which fell 6.32% or 100.40 points to trade at 1,487.85 at the close.  Wipro Ltd  (NSE:WIPR) declined 5.72% or 16.85 points to end at 277.65 and  IndusInd Bank  Ltd. (NSE:INBK) was down 5.41% or 56.60 points to 990.10. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were HDFC Bank Ltd (BO:HDBK) which rose 1.86% to 1,731.10, Axis Bank Ltd. (BO:AXBK) which was down 0.04% to settle at 1,015.85 and Reliance Industries Ltd (BO:RELI) which lost 0.62% to close at 1,199.60. Gold Futures for April delivery was down 1.63% or 47.50 to $2,862.20 a troy ounce.
