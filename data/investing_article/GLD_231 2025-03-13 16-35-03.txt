Investing.com – Denmark stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Consumer Goods, Real Estate and Healthcare sectors led shares higher. At the close in Copenhagen, the OMX Copenhagen 20 added 1.50%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Copenhagen 20 were  Novo Nordisk  A/S Class B (CSE:NOVOb), which rose 3.43% or 17.40 points to trade at 525.00 at the close. Meanwhile, Tryg A/S (CSE:TRYG) added 1.08% or 1.70 points to end at 159.30 and  Vestas Wind  Systems A/S (CSE:VWS) was up 0.87% or 0.90 points to 104.30 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Zealand Pharma  A/S (CSE:ZELA), which fell 10.68% or 72.00 points to trade at 602.00 at the close.  Pandora  A/S (CSE:PNDORA) declined 1.55% or 17.50 points to end at 1,111.00 and AP Moeller - Maersk A/S B (CSE:MAERSKb) was down 1.29% or 160.00 points to 12,205.00. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Copenhagen Stock Exchange by 66 to 59 and 19 ended unchanged. Crude oil for April delivery was down 1.29% or 0.87 to $66.81 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in May fell 1.09% or 0.77 to hit $70.18 a barrel, while the April Gold Futures contract rose 1.60% or 47.04 to trade at $2,993.84 a troy ounce. USD/DKK was up 0.23% to 6.87, while EUR/DKK unchanged 0.03% to 7.46. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.15% at 103.75.
