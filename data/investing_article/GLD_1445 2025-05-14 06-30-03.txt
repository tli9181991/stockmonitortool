Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Energy, IT and Resources sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 gained 0.13% to hit a new 1-month high. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were  Appen Ltd  (ASX:APX), which rose 5.86% or 0.07 points to trade at 1.18 at the close. Meanwhile, Zip Co Ltd (ASX:ZIP) added 5.79% or 0.12 points to end at 2.10 and  Megaport Ltd  (ASX:MP1) was up 4.22% or 0.53 points to 13.09 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Insignia Financial Ltd (ASX:IFL), which fell 15.25% or 0.61 points to trade at 3.39 at the close. Polynovo Ltd (ASX:PNV) declined 9.28% or 0.16 points to end at 1.51 and Aristocrat Leisure Ltd (ASX:ALL) was down 8.28% or 5.64 points to 62.49. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 559 to 542 and 430 ended unchanged. The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was down 5.66% to 11.40 a new 1-month low. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 0.41% or 13.19 to $3,234.61 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 0.49% or 0.31 to hit $63.36 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 0.50% or 0.33 to trade at $66.30 a barrel.
