Investing.com – Indonesia stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Financials, Infrastructure and Agriculture sectors led shares higher. At the close in Jakarta, the IDX Composite Index rose 2.16%. The best performers of the session on the IDX Composite Index were Andalan Sakti Primaindo PT (JK:ASPI), which rose 34.48% or 50.00 points to trade at 195.00 at the close. Meanwhile,  Data Sinergitama Jaya Tbk PT  (JK:ELIT) added 28.72% or 54.00 points to end at 242.00 and Artha Mahiya Investama Tbk PT (JK:AIMS) was up 25.00% or 68.00 points to 340.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Bumi Teknokultura Unggul Tbk (JK:BTEK), which fell 14.29% or 1.00 points to trade at 6.00 at the close.  Graha Andrasentra Propertindo  Tbk PT (JK:JGLE) declined 14.29% or 1.00 points to end at 6.00 and Express Transindo Utama Tbk (JK:TAXI) was down 14.29% or 1.00 points to 6.00. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Jakarta Stock Exchange by 410 to 219 and 210 ended unchanged. Crude oil for April delivery was down 0.54% or 0.37 to $67.89 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in May fell 0.10% or 0.07 to hit $70.97 a barrel, while the April Gold Futures contract rose 0.32% or 9.49 to trade at $2,930.09 a troy ounce. USD/IDR was down 0.47% to 16,314.70, while AUD/IDR fell 0.45% to 10,229.80. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.55% at 105.11.
