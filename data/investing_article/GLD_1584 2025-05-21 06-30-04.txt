Australia stocks higher at close of trade; S&P/ASX 200 up 0.52%
Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Gold, Metals & Mining and Resources sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 rose 0.52% to hit a new 3-months high. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were  Perseus Mining Ltd  (ASX:PRU), which rose 9.73% or 0.33 points to trade at 3.72 at the close. Meanwhile, Seek Ltd (ASX:SEK) added 7.09% or 1.57 points to end at 23.72 and  Regis Resources Ltd  (ASX:RRL) was up 6.92% or 0.32 points to 4.86 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Nufarm Ltd  (ASX:NUF), which fell 30.10% or 1.21 points to trade at 2.81 at the close.  Appen Ltd  (ASX:APX) declined 9.60% or 0.12 points to end at 1.13 and  Idp Education Ltd  (ASX:IEL) was down 6.55% or 0.59 points to 8.42. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 573 to 494 and 442 ended unchanged. Shares in Perseus Mining Ltd (ASX:PRU) rose to 5-year highs; rising 9.73% or 0.33 to 3.72. Shares in Nufarm Ltd (ASX:NUF) fell to 5-year lows; falling 30.10% or 1.21 to 2.81.  The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 1.11% to 11.60. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 1.10% or 35.99 to $3,320.59 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July rose 1.13% or 0.70 to hit $62.73 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract rose 1.04% or 0.68 to trade at $66.06 a barrel.
