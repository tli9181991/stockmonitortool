Investing.com – India stocks were higher after the close on Friday, as gains in the Oil & Gas, Public Sector Undertakings and Banking sectors led shares higher. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 rose 0.69% to hit a new 1-month high, while the BSE Sensex 30 index climbed 0.73%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  SBI Life Insurance  Company Ltd (NSE:SBIL), which rose 3.21% or 48.05 points to trade at 1,546.40 at the close. Meanwhile, Oil And Natural Gas Corporation Ltd (NSE:ONGC) added 2.90% or 6.83 points to end at 242.42 and  NTPC  Ltd (NSE:NTPC) was up 2.79% or 9.55 points to 351.30 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Hindalco Industries  Ltd. (NSE:HALC), which fell 1.50% or 10.60 points to trade at 695.35 at the close.  Infosys  Ltd (NSE:INFY) declined 1.42% or 23.00 points to end at 1,592.55 and  Wipro Ltd  (NSE:WIPR) was down 1.38% or 3.70 points to 264.30. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were NTPC Ltd (BO:NTPC) which rose 3.09% to 352.20, Bajaj Finance Ltd (BO:BJFN) which was up 2.62% to settle at 8,907.40 and Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. (BO:KTKM) which gained 2.14% to close at 2,078.90. The worst performers were Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd. (BO:MAHM) which was down 1.19% to 2,796.00 in late trade, Tata Steel Ltd (BO:TISC) which lost 1.19% to settle at 157.10 and Infosys Ltd (BO:INFY) which was down 0.98% to 1,598.30 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 1732 to 602 and 114 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 2690 rose and 1186 declined, while 121 ended unchanged. Shares in Bajaj Finance Ltd (BO:BJFN) rose to all time highs; rising 2.62% or 227.60 to 8,907.40. Shares in Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. (BO:KTKM) rose to 3-years highs; up 2.14% or 43.65 to 2,078.90.  The India VIX, which measures the implied volatility of Nifty 50 options, was down 0.24% to 12.57 a new 3-months low. Gold Futures for April delivery was down 0.21% or 6.51 to $3,037.29 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 0.32% or 0.22 to hit $67.85 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract fell 0.37% or 0.27 to trade at $71.73 a barrel.
