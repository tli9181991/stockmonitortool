Investing.com – Turkey stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Leasing & Factoring, Wood, Paper & Printing and Telecoms sectors led shares higher. At the close in Istanbul, the BIST 100 rose 0.01%. The best performers of the session on the BIST 100 were Ral Yatirim Holding AS (IS:RALYH), which rose 9.97% or 11.20 points to trade at 123.50 at the close. Meanwhile, Grainturk Holding AS (IS:GRTHO) added 5.35% or 14.00 points to end at 275.50 and  Cimsa  Cimento Sanayi ve Ticaret AS (IS:CIMSA) was up 4.68% or 2.35 points to 52.55 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Sekerbank  TAS (IS:SKBNK), which fell 4.56% or 0.26 points to trade at 5.44 at the close. Gubre Fabrikalari TAS (IS:GUBRF) declined 3.42% or 9.75 points to end at 275.00 and Ard Grup Bilisim Teknolojileri AS (IS:ARDYZ) was down 2.88% or 0.84 points to 28.28. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Istanbul Stock Exchange by 320 to 251 and 21 ended unchanged. Shares in Ral Yatirim Holding AS (IS:RALYH) rose to all time highs; up 9.97% or 11.20 to 123.50.  Gold Futures for June delivery was down 1.83% or 59.41 to $3,188.39 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 0.99% or 0.63 to hit $63.04 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 0.92% or 0.61 to trade at $66.02 a barrel. USD/TRY was down 0.06% to 38.75, while EUR/TRY rose 0.08% to 43.47. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.18% at 100.65.
