Investing.com – Brazil stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Real Estate, Consumption and Public Utilities sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa rose 1.79% to hit a new 6-months high. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were Hypera SA (BVMF:HYPE3), which rose 12.27% or 2.58 points to trade at 23.60 at the close. Meanwhile, Magazine Luiza SA (BVMF:MGLU3) added 10.80% or 1.03 points to end at 10.57 and  Pet Center Comercio e Partcipacoes  (BVMF:PETZ3) was up 9.65% or 0.39 points to 4.43 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Azul SA Pref  (BVMF:AZUL4), which fell 24.84% or 0.78 points to trade at 2.36 at the close. Minerva SA/Brazil (BVMF:BEEF3) declined 5.30% or 0.42 points to end at 7.50 and Usinas Siderurgicas de Minas Gerais SA USIMINAS Pref (BVMF:USIM5) was down 4.28% or 0.26 points to 5.81. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 658 to 266 and 55 ended unchanged. Shares in Azul SA Pref (BVMF:AZUL4) fell to all time lows; losing 24.84% or 0.78 to 2.36.
