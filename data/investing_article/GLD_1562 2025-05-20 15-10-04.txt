Investing.com – Morocco stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Banking, Beverage and Transport sectors led shares higher. At the close in  Casablanca , the Moroccan All Shares added 1.04%. The best performers of the session on the Moroccan All Shares were Travaux Generaux De Construction De Casablanca S.A (CSE:TGC), which rose 6.01% or 51.00 points to trade at 900.00 at the close. Meanwhile, Nationale d’Electrolyse et de Petrochimie Ste SA (CSE:SNP) added 3.03% or 20.00 points to end at 680.00 and Societe d’Exploitation des Ports SA (CSE:MSA) was up 2.87% or 22.00 points to 789.00 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Med Paper (CSE:MDP), which fell 5.99% or 1.35 points to trade at 21.20 at the close. Ste de Travaux de Realisation d’Ouvrages et de Constuction Industielle SA (CSE:STR) declined 3.92% or 2.19 points to end at 53.71 and Delta Holding S.A (CSE:DHO) was down 3.88% or 3.30 points to 81.70. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Casablanca Stock Exchange by 29 to 25 and 7 ended unchanged. Shares in Travaux Generaux De Construction De Casablanca S.A (CSE:TGC) rose to all time highs; gaining 6.01% or 51.00 to 900.00. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in July fell 0.56% or 0.37 to hit $65.17 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 1.37% or 44.39 to trade at $3,277.89 a troy ounce. EUR/MAD was down 0.06% to 10.41, while USD/MAD fell 0.11% to 9.25. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.22% at 100.07.
