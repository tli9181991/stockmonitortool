Investing.com – Australia stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Metals & Mining, Resources and Materials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 added 0.83%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Mineral Resources Ltd (ASX:MIN), which rose 11.57% or 2.54 points to trade at 24.49 at the close. Meanwhile,  Pilbara Minerals Ltd  (ASX:PLS) added 7.14% or 0.13 points to end at 1.95 and IGO Ltd (ASX:IGO) was up 7.27% or 0.29 points to 4.28 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Omni Bridgeway Ltd (ASX:OBL), which fell 4.04% or 0.06 points to trade at 1.31 at the close.  Appen Ltd  (ASX:APX) declined 3.60% or 0.05 points to end at 1.21 and Spark New Zealand Ltd (ASX:SPK) was down 3.59% or 0.07 points to 1.88. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 661 to 403 and 420 ended unchanged. Shares in Spark New Zealand Ltd (ASX:SPK) fell to 5-year lows; losing 3.59% or 0.07 to 1.88.  The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was down 5.02% to 15.36. Gold Futures for April delivery was down 0.28% or 8.51 to $2,992.59 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May rose 0.82% or 0.55 to hit $67.46 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract rose 0.85% or 0.60 to trade at $71.18 a barrel.
