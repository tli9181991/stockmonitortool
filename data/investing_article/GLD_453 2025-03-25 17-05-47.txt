Investing.com – France stocks were higher after the close on Tuesday, as gains in the Oil & Gas, Financials and Industrials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Paris, the CAC 40 gained 1.08%, while the SBF 120 index gained 1.04%. The best performers of the session on the CAC 40 were Airbus Group SE (EPA:AIR), which rose 3.05% or 5.10 points to trade at 172.32 at the close. Meanwhile,  BNP Paribas  SA (EPA:BNPP) added 2.71% or 2.15 points to end at 81.58 and  Credit Agricole  SA (EPA:CAGR) was up 2.64% or 0.45 points to 17.30 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Pernod Ricard  SA (EPA:PERP), which fell 1.04% or 0.98 points to trade at 93.20 at the close.  Legrand  SA (EPA:LEGD) declined 0.72% or 0.75 points to end at 103.95 and  Kering  SA (EPA:PRTP) was down 0.64% or 1.30 points to 201.10. The top performers on the SBF 120 were Solutions 30 SE (EPA:S30) which rose 9.73% to 1.73, Atos SE (EPA:ATOS) which was unchanged 5.26% to settle at 0.00 and  Valeo  SA (EPA:VLOF) which gained 4.10% to close at 10.25. The worst performers were Eutelsat Communications SA (EPA:ETL) which was down 11.61% to 3.41 in late trade,  Valneva  (EPA:VLS) which lost 6.58% to settle at 3.32 and  Alten  SA (EPA:LTEN) which was down 1.90% to 95.55 at the close. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Paris Stock Exchange by 309 to 182 and 75 ended unchanged. Shares in Pernod Ricard SA (EPA:PERP) fell to 5-year lows; down 1.04% or 0.98 to 93.20. Shares in BNP Paribas SA (EPA:BNPP) rose to 5-year highs; gaining 2.71% or 2.15 to 81.58. Shares in Credit Agricole SA (EPA:CAGR) rose to 5-year highs; gaining 2.64% or 0.45 to 17.30. Shares in Kering SA (EPA:PRTP) fell to 5-year lows; falling 0.64% or 1.30 to 201.10.  The CAC 40 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of CAC 40 options, was unchanged 0.00% to 18.96 a new 52-week high. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.43% or 13.14 to $3,057.14 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 0.36% or 0.25 to hit $68.86 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract fell 0.21% or 0.15 to trade at $72.22 a barrel.
