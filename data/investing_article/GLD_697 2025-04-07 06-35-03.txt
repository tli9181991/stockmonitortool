Investing.com – Australia stocks were lower after the close on Monday, as losses in the Energy, IT and Consumer Discretionary sectors led shares lower. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 declined 6.57% to hit a new 52-week low. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Challenger Ltd (ASX:CGF), which rose 2.39% or 0.14 points to trade at 6.00 at the close. Meanwhile, Reece Ltd (ASX:REH) added 0.68% or 0.10 points to end at 14.75 and AMP Ltd (ASX:AMP) was up 3.60% or 0.04 points to 1.15 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were St Barbara Ltd (ASX:SBM), which fell 14.89% or 0.04 points to trade at 0.20 at the close. Mineral Resources Ltd (ASX:MIN) declined 11.77% or 2.23 points to end at 16.72 and EML Payments Ltd (ASX:EML) was down 16.67% or 0.16 points to 0.80. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 1270 to 100 and 263 ended unchanged. Shares in Mineral Resources Ltd (ASX:MIN) fell to 3-years lows; losing 11.77% or 2.23 to 16.72.  The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 86.19% to 26.89 a new 3-years high. Gold Futures for June delivery was up 0.17% or 5.29 to $3,040.69 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 3.61% or 2.24 to hit $59.75 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract fell 3.52% or 2.31 to trade at $63.27 a barrel. AUD/USD was unchanged 0.48% to 0.60, while AUD/JPY fell 1.39% to 87.51. The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.44% at 102.32.
