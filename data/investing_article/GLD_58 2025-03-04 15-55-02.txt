Investing.com – Greece stocks were lower after the close on Tuesday, as losses in the Telecoms, Household and Basic Resources sectors led shares lower. At the close in Athens, the Athens General Composite fell 0.35%. The best performers of the session on the Athens General Composite were  Piraeus Port  (AT:OLPr), which rose 2.17% or 0.70 points to trade at 33.00 at the close. Meanwhile,  Athens International Airport  SA (AT:AIAr) added 1.95% or 0.17 points to end at 8.91 and  Alpha Bank  SA (AT:ACBr) was up 1.69% or 0.03 points to 1.98 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Avax SA (AT:AVAr), which fell 5.22% or 0.12 points to trade at 2.18 at the close.  Iktinos Hellas  (AT:IKTr) declined 5.01% or 0.02 points to end at 0.34 and Interlife General Insurance Company SA (AT:INLIFr) was down 3.67% or 0.18 points to 4.73. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Athens Stock Exchange by 87 to 37 and 18 ended unchanged. Shares in Alpha Bank SA (AT:ACBr) rose to 5-year highs; gaining 1.69% or 0.03 to 1.98. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April fell 1.43% or 0.98 to hit $67.39 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract fell 1.90% or 1.36 to trade at $70.26 a barrel.
