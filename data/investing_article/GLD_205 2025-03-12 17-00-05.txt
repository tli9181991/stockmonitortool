Investing.com – Finland stocks were lower after the close on Wednesday, as losses in the Utilities, Oil & Gas and Basic Materials sectors led shares lower. At the close in Helsinki, the OMX Helsinki 25 lost 0.25%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Helsinki 25 were Metso Oyj (HE:METSO), which rose 2.11% or 0.22 points to trade at 10.90 at the close. Meanwhile, Orion Oyj B (HE:ORNBV) added 2.04% or 1.08 points to end at 54.14 and  Mandatum Oyj  (HE:MANTA) was up 0.92% or 0.05 points to 5.49 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Outokumpu Oyj (HE:OUT1V), which fell 2.52% or 0.09 points to trade at 3.59 at the close.  Fortum  Oyj (HE:FORTUM) declined 2.11% or 0.33 points to end at 15.34 and Valmet Oyj (HE:VALMT) was down 1.89% or 0.52 points to 26.98. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Helsinki Stock Exchange by 93 to 71 and 23 ended unchanged. Brent oil for May delivery was up 1.94% or 1.35 to $70.91 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April rose 2.19% or 1.45 to hit $67.70 a barrel, while the April Gold Futures contract rose 0.81% or 23.69 to trade at $2,944.59 a troy ounce. EUR/USD was unchanged 0.11% to 1.09, while EUR/GBP unchanged 0.27% to 0.84. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.17% at 103.45.
