Investing.com – India stocks were lower after the close on Friday, as losses in the Technology, Auto and Public Sector Undertakings sectors led shares lower. At the close in NSE, the Nifty 50 fell 1.88% to hit a new 6-months low, while the BSE Sensex 30 index lost 1.90%. The best performers of the session on the Nifty 50 were  HDFC Bank Ltd  (NSE:HDBK), which rose 1.86% or 31.70 points to trade at 1,732.40 at the close. Meanwhile, Shriram Finance Ltd. (NSE:SHMF) added 1.73% or 10.50 points to end at 617.30 and  Coal India  Ltd (NSE:COAL) was up 1.51% or 5.50 points to 369.35 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Tech Mahindra  Ltd (NSE:TEML), which fell 6.32% or 100.40 points to trade at 1,487.85 at the close.  Wipro Ltd  (NSE:WIPR) declined 5.72% or 16.85 points to end at 277.65 and  IndusInd Bank  Ltd. (NSE:INBK) was down 5.41% or 56.60 points to 990.10. The top performers on the BSE Sensex 30 were HDFC Bank Ltd (BO:HDBK) which rose 1.79% to 1,729.90, Axis Bank Ltd. (BO:AXBK) which was down 0.32% to settle at 1,013.00 and Adani Port and Special Economic Zone Ltd (BO:APSE) which lost 0.60% to close at 1,071.95. The worst performers were IndusInd Bank Ltd. (BO:INBK) which was down 7.02% to 972.80 in late trade, Tech Mahindra Ltd (BO:TEML) which lost 6.38% to settle at 1,486.00 and Bharti Airtel Ltd (BO:BRTI) which was down 5.07% to 1,566.00 at the close. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the India National Stock Exchange by 2029 to 296 and 85 ended unchanged; on the Bombay Stock Exchange, 3077 fell and 747 advanced, while 90 ended unchanged. The India VIX, which measures the implied volatility of Nifty 50 options, was up 4.40% to 13.89. Gold Futures for April delivery was down 0.72% or 20.76 to $2,875.14 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April fell 1.14% or 0.80 to hit $69.55 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract fell 1.03% or 0.76 to trade at $72.81 a barrel.
