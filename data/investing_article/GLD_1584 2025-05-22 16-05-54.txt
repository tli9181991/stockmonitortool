France stocks lower at close of trade; CAC 40 down 0.58%
Investing.com – France stocks were lower after the close on Thursday, as losses in the Consumer Goods, Oil & Gas and Technology sectors led shares lower. At the close in Paris, the CAC 40 fell 0.58%, while the SBF 120 index lost 0.57%. The best performers of the session on the CAC 40 were  Thales  (EPA:TCFP), which rose 1.65% or 4.20 points to trade at 259.50 at the close. Meanwhile, EssilorLuxottica SA (EPA:ESLX) added 0.84% or 2.20 points to end at 262.60 and Orange SA (EPA:ORAN) was up 0.71% or 0.10 points to 13.43 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were Stellantis NV (EPA:STLAM), which fell 3.84% or 0.37 points to trade at 9.17 at the close. ArcelorMittal SA (AS:MT) declined 3.79% or 1.05 points to end at 26.66 and  Renault  SA (EPA:RENA) was down 2.81% or 1.39 points to 48.15. The top performers on the SBF 120 were  Elior  Group (EPA:ELIOR) which rose 3.84% to 2.81,  Elis Services SA  (EPA:ELIS) which was up 2.42% to settle at 23.74 and  Coface  (EPA:COFA) which gained 2.04% to close at 16.49. The worst performers were  Worldline SA  (EPA:WLN) which was down 6.05% to 4.88 in late trade, Eutelsat Communications SA (EPA:ETL) which lost 5.67% to settle at 3.25 and  Ubisoft  Entertainment (EPA:UBIP) which was down 4.69% to 9.85 at the close. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Paris Stock Exchange by 302 to 182 and 95 ended unchanged. Shares in Orange SA (EPA:ORAN) rose to 5-year highs; gaining 0.71% or 0.10 to 13.43. Shares in Elis Services SA (EPA:ELIS) rose to all time highs; up 2.42% or 0.56 to 23.74.  The CAC 40 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of CAC 40 options, was unchanged 0.00% to 18.96 a new 52-week high. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 0.57% or 18.89 to $3,294.61 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in July fell 1.27% or 0.78 to hit $60.79 a barrel, while the July Brent oil contract fell 1.36% or 0.88 to trade at $64.03 a barrel.
