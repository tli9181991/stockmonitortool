Investing.com – Finland stocks were higher after the close on Monday, as gains in the Consumer Services, Oil & Gas and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Helsinki, the OMX Helsinki 25 gained 0.17% to hit a new 1-month high. The best performers of the session on the OMX Helsinki 25 were Neste Oyj (HE:NESTE), which rose 1.95% or 0.18 points to trade at 9.51 at the close. Meanwhile, Hiab Oyj (HE:HIAB) added 0.99% or 0.42 points to end at 42.88 and Sampo Oyj A (HE:SAMPO) was up 0.78% or 0.07 points to 9.03 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Fortum  Oyj (HE:FORTUM), which fell 1.48% or 0.22 points to trade at 14.33 at the close.  Konecranes  ABP (HE:KCRA) declined 1.43% or 0.85 points to end at 58.70 and Outokumpu Oyj (HE:OUT1V) was down 1.16% or 0.04 points to 3.40. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Helsinki Stock Exchange by 102 to 73 and 12 ended unchanged. Shares in Sampo Oyj A (HE:SAMPO) rose to 52-week highs; gaining 0.78% or 0.07 to 9.03.  Brent oil for July delivery was down 2.12% or 1.30 to $59.99 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in June fell 2.38% or 1.39 to hit $56.90 a barrel, while the June Gold Futures contract rose 2.38% or 77.31 to trade at $3,320.61 a troy ounce.
