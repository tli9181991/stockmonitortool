Investing.com – Russia stocks were lower after the close on Thursday, as losses in the Mining, Telecoms and Power sectors led shares lower. At the close in Moscow, the MOEX Russia Index fell 2.18% to hit a new 1-month low. The best performers of the session on the MOEX Russia Index were Aeroflot PJSC (MCX:AFLT), which rose 4.48% or 3.02 points to trade at 70.45 at the close. Meanwhile, Unipro PJSC (MCX:UPRO) added 2.15% or 0.04 points to end at 1.85 and AFK Sistema PJSC (MCX:AFKS) was up 1.79% or 0.29 points to 16.28 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  PhosAgro  PJSC (MCX:PHOR), which fell 5.07% or 322.00 points to trade at 6,023.00 at the close. Rostelekom PJSC (MCX:RTKM) declined 4.16% or 2.72 points to end at 62.70 and Moskovskiy Kreditnyi Bank PAO (MCX:CBOM) was down 3.87% or 0.35 points to 8.57. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Moscow Stock Exchange by 221 to 28 and 4 ended unchanged. The Russian Volatility Index – RVI, which measures the implied volatility of MOEX Russia Index options, was up 4.79% to 52.10. Gold Futures for June delivery was down 0.88% or 27.95 to $3,138.25 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in May fell 7.10% or 5.09 to hit $66.62 a barrel, while the June Brent oil contract rose 0.06% or 0.04 to trade at $69.90 a barrel.
