Investing.com – Finland stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Basic Materials, Oil & Gas and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Helsinki, the OMX Helsinki 25 rose 1.36%. The best performers of the session on the OMX Helsinki 25 were Metso Oyj (HE:METSO), which rose 5.98% or 0.61 points to trade at 10.81 at the close. Meanwhile,  Konecranes  ABP (HE:KCRA) added 4.82% or 3.20 points to end at 69.65 and Valmet Oyj (HE:VALMT) was up 4.49% or 1.17 points to 27.25 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Kojamo  (HE:KOJAMO), which fell 3.59% or 0.33 points to trade at 8.88 at the close. Orion Oyj B (HE:ORNBV) declined 2.04% or 1.14 points to end at 54.64 and KONE Oyj (HE:KNEBV) was down 1.67% or 0.92 points to 54.08. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the Helsinki Stock Exchange by 122 to 50 and 17 ended unchanged. Brent oil for May delivery was down 3.49% or 2.48 to $68.56 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April fell 4.12% or 2.81 to hit $65.45 a barrel, while the April Gold Futures contract rose 0.47% or 13.66 to trade at $2,934.26 a troy ounce.
