Investing.com – Australia stocks were lower after the close on Tuesday, as losses in the IT, Gold and Industrials sectors led shares lower. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 declined 0.91% to hit a new 6-months low. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were Resmed Inc DRC (ASX:RMD), which rose 3.11% or 1.13 points to trade at 37.58 at the close. Meanwhile, Cleanaway Waste Management Ltd (ASX:CWY) added 2.17% or 0.06 points to end at 2.60 and Domain Holdings Australia Ltd (ASX:DHG) was up 2.04% or 0.09 points to 4.50 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Nickel Mines  Ltd (ASX:NIC), which fell 18.21% or 0.14 points to trade at 0.62 at the close.  Ramelius Resources  Ltd (ASX:RMS) declined 16.85% or 0.47 points to end at 2.32 and  Pro Medicus Ltd  (ASX:PME) was down 10.58% or 27.07 points to 228.86. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 912 to 208 and 314 ended unchanged. Shares in Nickel Mines Ltd (ASX:NIC) fell to 52-week lows; losing 18.21% or 0.14 to 0.62. Shares in Domain Holdings Australia Ltd (ASX:DHG) rose to 3-years highs; rising 2.04% or 0.09 to 4.50.  The S&P/ASX 200 VIX, which measures the implied volatility of S&P/ASX 200 options, was up 6.59% to 15.26 a new 6-months high. Gold Futures for April delivery was up 0.07% or 2.09 to $2,901.49 a troy ounce. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Crude oil for delivery in April fell 0.14% or 0.09 to hit $65.94 a barrel, while the May Brent oil contract fell 0.03% or 0.02 to trade at $69.26 a barrel.
