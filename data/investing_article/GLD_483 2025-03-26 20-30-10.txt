Investing.com – Brazil stocks were higher after the close on Wednesday, as gains in the Real Estate, Basic Materials and Financials sectors led shares higher. At the close in Sao Paulo, the Bovespa added 0.34% to hit a new 3-months high. The best performers of the session on the Bovespa were Braskem SA (BVMF:BRKM5), which rose 9.68% or 1.04 points to trade at 11.78 at the close. The worst performers of the session were Automob Participações SA (BVMF:AMOB3), which fell 7.41% or 0.02 points to trade at 0.25 at the close. Minerva SA/Brazil (BVMF:BEEF3) declined 3.18% or 0.19 points to end at 5.79 and JBS SA (BVMF:JBSS3) was down 2.70% or 1.10 points to 39.64. Rising stocks outnumbered declining ones on the B3 Stock Exchange by 486 to 444 and 58 ended unchanged. USD/BRL was up 0.63% to 5.73, while EUR/BRL rose 0.31% to 6.17. The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.37% at 104.22.
