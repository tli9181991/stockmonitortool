Investing.com – Australia stocks were lower after the close on Friday, as losses in the Healthcare, Utilities and Energy sectors led shares lower. At the close in Sydney, the S&P/ASX 200 fell 0.82%. The best performers of the session on the S&P/ASX 200 were St Barbara Ltd (ASX:SBM), which rose 9.52% or 0.02 points to trade at 0.23 at the close. Meanwhile,  Evolution Mining  Ltd (ASX:EVN) added 7.84% or 0.56 points to end at 7.70 and  Northern Star Resources  Ltd (ASX:NST) was up 5.52% or 1.12 points to 21.40 in late trade. The worst performers of the session were  Mesoblast  Ltd (ASX:MSB), which fell 9.22% or 0.16 points to trade at 1.58 at the close. Tabcorp Holdings Ltd (ASX:TAH) declined 5.88% or 0.04 points to end at 0.56 and Nuix Ltd (ASX:NXL) was down 5.24% or 0.13 points to 2.35. Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Sydney Stock Exchange by 583 to 485 and 405 ended unchanged. Shares in Evolution Mining Ltd (ASX:EVN) rose to all time highs; up 7.84% or 0.56 to 7.70. Shares in Northern Star Resources Ltd (ASX:NST) rose to all time highs; gaining 5.52% or 1.12 to 21.40.
